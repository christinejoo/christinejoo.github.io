Reading Notes — 
NFTs, or the Readymade Reversed, Reading Notes

Marcel Duchamp's readymade work introduced ideas of desublimation and abandonment. A non-fungible token (NFT) is dependent upon right to control its distribution. A quote that sums up Duchamp's connection to NFTs is "It bears repeating: Duchamp used the category of art to liberate materiality from commodifiable form; the NFT deploys the category of art to extract private property from freely available information." NFTs dismiss the tangible experience of viewing art.

Reading Notes — 
Conditional Design, Moniker

The Conditional Design Manifesto emphasizes the creative process rather than the end products and how the process is technically the product. Change over time and relationships are common themes. A quote is "In this way design shifts from being a different series of styles and techniques more or less appropriate to a particular problem to a process and method for solving not singular but recurrent problems." Sol LeWitt used instruction-based art as a tool for creating tools.

A Handmade Web, J.R. Carpenter

Handmade web refers to web pages coded by hand, they challenge conventions through a personal aesthetic. The handmade web refers to manual labor of the physical body — through work by the hand (coding), brower becomes real. "Ironically, handheld devices have distanced us from the handmade web."

This reading makes me think about the style of a lot of Parsons faculty and students, pushing the expressivity of code more than the commerical aesthetic. Also, thinking about exploring different possibilites of the web especially as a student.
